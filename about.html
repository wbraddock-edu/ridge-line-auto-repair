Our Story

About Ridge Line Auto

Three generations of mechanics. One simple belief: fix what's broken, charge what's fair, and tell the truth every time.

HomeAbout
Owner & Master Technician

Dale Pruitt

Dale Pruitt grew up watching his grandfather, Earl Pruitt, work in a garage. Earl had opened a one-bay shop just outside Narrows in 1962 — a hand-painted sign, a used lift, and a reputation for honest work that spread across Giles County faster than he ever expected.

Dale's father carried on that tradition through the '80s and '90s, and Dale spent his teenage years handing tools and learning the fundamentals. After earning his ASE certifications and spending five years working at a regional dealership, Dale came home in 2005 and opened Ridge Line Auto Repair on Main Street with one goal: bring dealership-level skill to a shop with small-town values.

"I got tired of watching customers get upsold on things they didn't need. The dealership was good at repairs — but the culture wasn't right. I wanted to run a shop where every customer leaves knowing exactly what was done to their car, why, and what it cost. No mystery, no pressure."

— Dale Pruitt, Owner

Community

Rooted in This Community

Ridge Line Auto Repair is more than a business — it's a Narrows institution. Dale grew up here, raised his family here, and has invested in this community for more than two decades.

We sponsor the Narrows Eagles youth baseball and softball programs, support the annual Giles County fair, and donate labor to the county's elderly assistance program each year — free oil changes for seniors on fixed incomes.

"These are my neighbors. When I send someone down the road with their car fixed, I'm going to see them at the grocery store, at church, at the game on Friday night. That's accountability you can't put a price on."
